8.1 Installation of permanent traffic control devices

The ConOps should include a use case that explains the process for installing and removing a permanent traffic control device (e.g., a parking sign) and explain how the process needs to work in real time to ensure consistency in information, especially when a traffic control device installation does not occur on schedule and the system needs to flag the discrepancy. And also perhaps for what happens with a missing device (e.g., missing speed limit sign as approaching or leaving a town on a rural highway) This should include a discussion of the update cycle of static rules, the activation of rules with dynamic data as signs are installed, and the provisioning of dynamic data when the status of rules are condition specific.

Include a scenario that details the process to deploy (static, temporary) rules that follows a flow of: publicizing first (pull; perhaps with expected date) followed by activation of rule (with deployment of traffic control device) and announcement that the rule is active (push)

8.3 Operation of rules dependent upon dynamic data

The ConOps might include a scenario with a variable speed limit system that has a fixed schedule that can be overridden by events.

The scenario should use the terminology of "data" (e.g., sensors), "information" (processed data), and "delivery" (connections) to clearly indicate how it addresses these three key aspects of ITS deployments.

This example will provide a mixture of types of communications (e.g., low-latency push and high-latency pull)

8.9 ADS fallback based on METR information

The ConOps should include an example where an ADS is required to fallback to manual mode when leaving METR-coverage. We should note that this is our assumption, but the exact rules are up to the jurisdiction and OEM to determine.

The ConOps should include a scenario of an ODD boundary where a ADS-equipped vehicle needs to navigate to an area where it can reach achieve a "minimal risk condition". The scenario should provide examples that the jurisdiction (or private campus) might have rules prohibiting temporary parking and the ADS-equipped vehicle will need to navigate to an appropriate location.

8.10 Starting outside of METR coverage

The ConOps should include a scenario where a vehicle starts in an area without any connectivity. This scenario should include a note that we assume that for the near future, every ADS-equipped vehicle will have a manual drive mode (or be small enough to easily transport). If not, care should be taken to ensure that the rules do not expire while the car is parked, because any remote refresh would have to be performed by a trusted source and it is not yet determined what the requirements are for such a trusted source.
